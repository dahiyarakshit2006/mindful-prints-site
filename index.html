Line 01

LabelsFlexographic · Custom · Security

Labels are where a product's compliance, traceability and shelf presence all land on the same piece of film. We print them to survive whatever comes next — cold chain, condensation, oil, abrasion, or a tamper attempt.

The range covers PCB labels for electronics assembly, Mylar labels for high-heat and chemical exposure, water-sensitive labels, chromo labels (removable, hi-tech and white), piggyback labels for multi-layer information, and security formats including ultra-destructible vinyl and void labels that leave visible evidence of removal.

Line 02

Thermal transfer ribbonsWax · Resin · Wax-resin

The ribbon decides whether a barcode still scans after six months in a warehouse. We supply all three chemistries and, more usefully, help you pick the right one for your substrate and printhead rather than selling you the cheapest roll.

Wax for paper labels and general logistics. Wax-resin where you need sharper edges and better smudge resistance on coated stock. Resin for synthetic films exposed to solvents, heat and abrasion — the standard for pharma, electronics and outdoor assets.

Product rangeFasfilm TT Matt Silver · Fasfilm TT White · TT Superior · TTE · Fastrans PET Matte Chrome
ChemistriesWax · Wax-resin · Full resin
CompatibilityNear-edge and flat-head thermal transfer printers from all major brands; supplied in matched core sizes and widths
OrderingCut to width and length on request; ribbon–label pairing tested before dispatch

Line 03 — New

Rigid boxesStructural · Premium · Made to order

A rigid box is the only part of the product a customer holds before they hold the product. Ours are built on greyboard, hand-wrapped, and engineered around the item — not around a stock size that happens to be close.

We design the structure in-house: custom die-cut development, drop-tested inserts, and a physical white-sample before tooling. Closures range from simple lid-and-base to magnetic flap, book-style and drawer formats. Finishes include foil stamping, embossing and debossing, and spot UV over matte lamination.

Minimum order quantities are kept flexible — we run short pilot batches for launches and festive SKUs, then scale the same tooling for volume.

Line 04 — New

Protective filmSurface protection · Transit · Retail display

Most surface damage happens between the line and the shelf. Protective film is the cheapest insurance against it: a temporary skin that takes the scratches, then peels away clean without residue or ghosting.

We supply PE protective film in low-tack through high-tack adhesive grades, plus static-cling film where no adhesive is permitted at all. Adhesion is specified to the surface — a brushed stainless panel and a coated automotive trim need very different peel values, and getting it wrong means either lift-off in transit or residue on removal.

Film can be custom printed: brand marks for retail display units, QC batch codes and line markings, or tamper-evident messaging that shows the film has been lifted.

TypesPE protective film · Static-cling (adhesive-free) · Low-tack, medium-tack, high-tack variants
ApplicationsScreen and lens protection · Brushed metal and appliance panels · Automotive trim · Furniture laminates and profiles
PerformanceSpecified adhesion levels · UV-resistant grades for outdoor duration · Residue-free clean removal
PrintingCustom print for branding, QC batch marking and tamper evidence
SupplyRolls slit to width; sheeted formats on request

Line 05 — New

Phone packagingIntegrated · Tamper-evident · At scale

Phone packaging is where our other three lines meet. A single handset box needs a rigid structure, a protective inner, a scannable IMEI label and a seal that proves the box has never been opened. Sourcing those from four vendors is how batches end up mismatched.

We deliver the whole set as one specification: the retail rigid box, the foam or pulp inner that holds the handset and accessories, the protective film on screen and body, and the label and barcode integration carrying IMEI, model, batch and QC data — plus tamper-evident seals in void or ultra-destructible material.

Built for OEMs, importers and wholesalers, and refurbished-phone sellers who need packaging that is brand-consistent, compliant and impossible to reseal unnoticed.

Line 06

Barcoding & die-cuttingTraceability · Precision converting

A barcode that fails to scan at a distributor's dock is a returned consignment. We print and verify barcode labels for grade and contrast, and supply the scanning hardware pairing where you need it.

On the converting side, our precision die-cutting covers custom label profiles, complex box shapes, window cut-outs and multi-layer piggyback constructions — tooled to your artwork with tight registration to print.
